# Panel Protocol

Each persona writes an isolated pass with:

- evidence ids used
- strongest argument for
- strongest argument against
- core contribution assessment: whether there is one defensible scientific contribution or only engineering activity
- causal identity and closest competing idea
- mechanism and predicted-pattern difference from that competitor
- cheapest discriminating experiment
- unique decision targets, cheapest-falsifier GPU-hours, and the evidence basis
  for that estimate
- reusable baseline/data/runtime invariants and reviewer novelty/confound risks
- storyline readiness for shortlisted ideas; require the complete storyline only for the selected primary
- weakest assumption
- falsifier pilot
- advance/park/kill recommendation

The Researcher first generates 8-12 lightweight cards, merges matching causal
signatures, and then synthesizes one deterministic 3-5 idea shortlist for the
selection revision. Deep review applies only to that shortlist. Ordinary
heartbeats consume it in batch and do not rerun the panel. Tournament scores are
screening evidence, not novelty or launch authority; use no invented success
probability.
